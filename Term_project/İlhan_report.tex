\section{Methods Implemented (M2 + M3 + M4 combined)}

\textbf{Pilot Pattern Rearrangement (TX modification) }
The default \texttt{dense\_4sym} pattern places pilots only on symbols \{0,3,6,9\},
creating a 3-symbol gap of 130\,\textmu s. At $f_d=3000$\,Hz,
$J_0(2\pi \cdot 3000 \cdot 130\times10^{-6}) \approx 0$ ---
the channel is fully decorrelated between snapshots, making any interpolation useless.
I changed to \textbf{comb\_10sym\_64}: 64 pilots distributed across \emph{all} 10 symbols
(even RBs: 3 pilots/RB at 4-SC spacing; odd RBs: 2 pilots/RB at 6-SC spacing;
average 4.9\,SC spacing, 248 data SCs).
The reduced gap of 43\,\textmu s gives $J_0(2\pi\cdot3000\cdot43\times10^{-6})\approx0.84$
--- strong correlation every symbol. The 64-pilot cap is respected exactly.

\textbf{Residual STO Correction (M3 --- Classen--Meyr phase-slope estimator).}
I implemented a frequency-domain differential phase estimator.
A residual STO of $\delta$ samples imprints a linear phase ramp
$e^{-j2\pi k\delta/N_\text{FFT}}$ across subcarriers.
For each consecutive pilot pair with known frequency spacing $\Delta k$,
$\delta = -\Delta\phi \cdot N_\text{FFT}/(2\pi\,\Delta k)$.
Averaging over all valid pairs across all 10 symbols gives a robust estimate.
This was chosen over CP self-correlation because the CP correlator has a
flat-top ambiguity that makes it unreliable for small offsets.
Unambiguous range is $\pm128$ samples, exceeding the $\pm32$-sample spec.

\textbf{Residual CFO Correction (M2 --- van de Beek CP correlation).}
I used the \textbf{van de Beek CP self-correlation} estimator.
A CFO $\Delta f$ introduces a phase shift between the CP and its tail copy
proportional to $2\pi\,\Delta f\,N_\text{FFT}/F_s$.
Averaging over all 10 symbols gives range $\pm15$\,kHz, covering $\pm12$\,kHz.
The method is unbiased under multipath because all channel taps (max 12 samples)
fall well within the 288-sample CP.

\textbf{Doppler-Aware Channel Estimation (M3 --- scattered pilots + Wiener smoother).}
Two upgrades over LS + nearest-neighbour:
(1)~Cubic \textbf{spline} frequency interpolation handles the non-uniform
4/6\,SC pilot spacing better than linear.
(2)~\textbf{Wiener time smoother} applied to all 10 LS pilot snapshots before interpolation:
$\mathbf{W} = \mathbf{R}_{HH}(\mathbf{R}_{HH}+\sigma^2\mathbf{I})^{-1}$,
where $R_{HH}(i,j)=J_0(2\pi f_d|i-j|T_\text{sym})$ encodes Jakes temporal correlation.
At $f_d=0$ this averages all snapshots (noise $\downarrow\!\sqrt{10}$);
at $f_d=3000$\,Hz it acts as a Jakes-shaped low-pass filter.
$\sigma^2=5\times10^{-4}$ is tuned to the 30\,dB grading point.

\textbf{ICI-Aware Equalization (M4 --- extended to Q=6 neighbours).}
At $f_d=3000$\,Hz the channel varies within one symbol ($T_u\approx33\,\mu$s),
causing inter-carrier interference.
After initial ZF equalization, the channel slope $\Delta H$ per symbol is estimated from
central differences of adjacent symbol H snapshots.
ICI weights $w(\Delta k)=1/\bigl(N(e^{-j2\pi\Delta k/N}-1)\bigr)$ are derived
from the DFT of the linear time ramp.
Hard QPSK decisions are used to subtract estimated ICI from $Y$ before
re-equalization, truncated to $Q=6$ neighbours ($\approx$94\% of ICI energy).

% -------------------------------------------------------------------------
\textbf{M1 not used.} M1's Schmidl--Cox/Moose CFO estimator requires a preamble.
Since this project operates on the data frame only (no preamble), M1's CFO mechanism
is inapplicable. The Doppler tracking idea from M1 (per-symbol channel re-estimation
from comb pilots) is adopted through the comb\_10sym\_64 pilot pattern.

\section{What Worked and What Didn't}

The pilot rearrangement to \texttt{comb\_10sym\_64} was the single most impactful change,
eliminating the Doppler dead zone caused by zero Jakes correlation in \texttt{dense\_4sym}.
The Wiener time smoother added +4\% complexity for 13--19\% BER reduction across all
Doppler values.
Several approaches were tried and reverted:
\emph{MMSE equalization} failed because LS estimation absorbs noise into the channel estimate
($\sigma^2\!\to\!0$), so the dominant error is interpolation bias, not noise amplification.
\emph{Wiener frequency interpolation} failed because the 711-bin DC/guard gap between
the two active frequency halves makes the cross-half correlation matrix near-singular
(coherence BW $\approx$400\,kHz vs.\ 9.2\,MHz gap).
\emph{Pilot-based CFO refinement} worsened performance by adding $\sim$200\,Hz Doppler
phase noise, larger than the ${<}50$\,Hz van de Beek residual.
\emph{Decision-directed channel refinement} at all 312\,SCs showed negligible gain because
the dominant residual at $f_d=3000$\,Hz is non-linear Jakes variation within the symbol ---
a frequency-domain refinement does not address a time-domain problem.
